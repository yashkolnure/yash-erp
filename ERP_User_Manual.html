1 Getting Started

Everything you need to know before using the ERP system for the first time.

Logging In

Open your browser and navigate to the ERP application URL provided by your administrator. You will see the Login page.

  1. 1
    Enter your Email AddressType the email address registered for your account.
  2. 2
    Enter your PasswordType your password. First-time users should use the temporary password provided by their administrator.
  3. 3
    Click "Sign In"You will be redirected to the Dashboard on success.
Tip: If you forget your password, contact your system administrator to reset it. You can change your password any time via Admin Settings → Change Password.

Navigating the Interface

The ERP interface has three main areas:

  • Sidebar (left): Main navigation menu organized by module. Click a group header to expand/collapse it. Click any item to navigate to that page.
  • Header (top): Shows the current company name, a notification bell, and your profile menu (logout, change password).
  • Content area (center): The active page or module you are working in.
Note: Some sidebar items may be hidden depending on your role and permissions. Contact your administrator if you cannot see a module you need.

Selecting Your Company

If your account belongs to multiple companies, a company selector appears at the top of the sidebar. Click it to switch companies. All data is strictly separated per company — switching companies will reload all data for the selected company.

2 Dashboard

The Dashboard gives you a real-time financial overview of your business. It is the first screen you see after logging in.

What the Dashboard Shows

The dashboard is divided into several sections:

  • KPI Cards (top row): Four key metrics — Total Revenue, Outstanding AR, Total Expenses, and Active Employees — each showing a trend indicator (up/down).
  • Revenue Trend Chart: An area chart showing monthly invoice revenue for the selected date range. If no invoices exist yet, a friendly placeholder is shown.
  • Invoice Status Distribution (pie chart): A breakdown of invoices by status — Draft, Posted, Partially Paid, Paid, Overdue, Cancelled.
  • Recent Invoices table: The 5 most recent invoices with customer, amount, and status.
  • Low Stock Alerts: Products whose current stock has fallen below their reorder level.
  • Pending Approvals: A count of approvals waiting for your action.

Filtering by Date Range

  1. 1
    Set From DateClick the "From" date field in the top-right of the dashboard header and type or select a date.
  2. 2
    Set To DateClick the "To" date field and select the end date.
  3. 3
    Click "Apply"The KPI cards, revenue chart, and invoice table all refresh to show data for that date range only. Default is the last 12 months.
Tip: Click any metric card or the "View All" link on the Recent Invoices table to navigate directly to the relevant module.

3 Sales & CRM

Manage the full sales cycle — from capturing leads to collecting payment. The Sales & CRM module covers Leads, Customers, Quotations, Sales Orders, Invoices, and Credit Notes.

Leads & Pipeline (CRM)

SIDEBAR → Sales & CRM → Leads & Pipeline

Track prospective customers through a structured sales pipeline. Each lead moves through stages from initial contact to a closed deal.

Creating a Lead

  1. 1

  2. 2

    FieldRequiredDescription
    NameRequiredLead or contact person's name

    EmailOptionalEmail for follow-up communication
    PhoneOptionalContact phone number

Using the Pipeline View

The pipeline view shows leads as cards grouped in columns by stage. You can see the count and total value at the top of each column. Click any lead card to open its detail view and add activities.

Adding Activities

With a lead open, scroll to the Activity section. Enter a note describing your interaction (call, email, meeting) and click "Add Activity". All activities appear in a chronological log.

Converting a Lead to a Customer

When a lead is won, click the "Convert to Customer" button inside the lead detail. This automatically creates a Customer record with the same contact details. The lead stage is updated to "Won".

Quotations

SIDEBAR → Sales & CRM → Quotations

Quotations are formal price offers sent to customers before a sale is confirmed.

Creating a Quotation

  1. 1
    Click "New Quotation"
  2. 2
    Select a CustomerStart typing to search from your customer list.
  3. 3
    Set Validity DateThe date until which the quote is valid for the customer.
  4. 4
    Add Line ItemsClick "Add Line" for each product/service. Enter the product, quantity, unit price, and applicable tax rate. Subtotals and totals calculate automatically.
  5. 5
    Add Terms & NotesOptional free-text terms and internal notes.
  6. 6
    SaveThe quotation is saved in "Draft" status.

Quotation Workflow

Draft→ Sent→ Accepted / Rejected→ Converted to Sales Order
  • Send: Click "Send Quotation" to mark it as Sent (triggers email notification to customer).
  • Accept / Reject: Record the customer's decision by clicking "Accept" or "Reject".
  • Convert: Click "Convert to Sales Order" on an accepted quotation to create a Sales Order automatically.
  • Duplicate: Click the Duplicate icon to create a copy of any quotation as a new Draft.

Sales Orders

SIDEBAR → Sales & CRM → Sales Orders

Sales Orders are confirmed commitments to sell goods or services to a customer. They can be created from a Quotation or directly.

Creating a Sales Order

The form is identical to the Quotation form — select customer, add line items (product, quantity, price, tax). Click "Save" to create a Draft order.

Sales Order Workflow

Draft→ Confirmed→ Invoiced
  • Confirm: Click "Confirm Order" to move from Draft to Confirmed. Stock is reserved at this point.
  • Create Invoice: Click "Create Invoice" on a confirmed order to automatically generate an AR Invoice with all the line items pre-populated.
  • Assign: Use the "Assign" button to assign the order to a team member for fulfilment.
Note: Once an invoice is created from a Sales Order, the order status changes to "Invoiced" and can no longer be modified.

Invoices (Accounts Receivable)

SIDEBAR → Sales & CRM → Invoices

AR Invoices are the bills you send to customers for goods or services delivered. This is the core of your receivables management.

Creating an Invoice

  1. 1
    Click "New Invoice"
  2. 2
    Select CustomerThe customer's default currency and payment terms are auto-filled.
  3. 3
    Set Invoice Date and Due Date
  4. 4
    Add Line ItemsProduct, description, quantity, price, tax%. Totals calculate live.
  5. 5
    Add Notes or TermsAppears on the printed invoice.
  6. 6
    Save as Draftor proceed to Post immediately.

Invoice Status Lifecycle

Draft→ Posted→ Partially Paid / Paid

Status descriptions:

StatusMeaning
DraftCreated but not yet posted. Can be freely edited.
PostedPosted to the general ledger. A journal entry has been created. Cannot be edited — can only be cancelled.
Partially PaidOne or more payments have been applied but the invoice is not fully settled.
PaidFully settled. No further payment needed.
OverduePast due date and not yet fully paid. Shown as a warning indicator.
CancelledVoided. A reversal journal entry is automatically created.

Posting an Invoice

Click "Post Invoice" on a Draft invoice. This creates the GL journal entry (Debit: Accounts Receivable, Credit: Revenue) and locks the invoice for editing. The accounting period must be Open for posting to succeed.

Printing / PDF Export

Open any invoice and click "Print / PDF". The browser print dialog opens with a clean, print-formatted invoice layout. Select "Save as PDF" in the print dialog to download a PDF copy.

Emailing an Invoice

Click "Send Email" to send the invoice to the customer's email address on file. A confirmation is shown when the email is dispatched.

Payment Reminder

For overdue invoices, click "Send Reminder". A payment reminder email is automatically sent to the customer with the outstanding amount and due date.

Duplicating an Invoice

Click the Duplicate button (copy icon) on any invoice to create a new Draft invoice with all the same line items, customer, and dates. Useful for recurring billing.

Bulk Actions

On the invoice list, check the box on the left of each row (or click the header checkbox to select all) to enable bulk actions:

  • Bulk Export: Download selected invoices as a CSV file.
  • Bulk Post: Post multiple Draft invoices at once.

Search & Filters

Use the search bar to find invoices by customer name or invoice number. Use the Status dropdown to filter by Draft, Posted, Paid, etc. Use the date range fields to narrow by invoice date.

File Attachments

Open any invoice, scroll to the Attachments section, and click "Upload File". Attach supporting documents (purchase orders, contracts, delivery notes). All attachments are stored securely and can be downloaded later.

Goods Receipt Notes (GRN)

SIDEBAR → Procurement → Goods Receipts

A Goods Receipt Note records the physical receipt of goods from a vendor against a Purchase Order. Creating a GRN automatically updates stock levels in the warehouse.

Creating a GRN

  1. 1
    Click "New GRN"
  2. 2
    Select the Purchase OrderAll line items from the PO are pre-populated.
  3. 3

  5. 5

  6. 6
    Save & PostStock levels are updated immediately upon posting.
Tip: Always create a GRN before recording a Purchase Bill. The GRN proves goods were received and is the basis for a 3-way match (PO → GRN → Bill).

Bank Reconciliation

SIDEBAR → Finance → Bank Reconciliation

Bank Reconciliation matches your bank statement transactions against the payments recorded in the ERP to ensure accuracy.

Starting a Reconciliation

  1. 1
    Click "New Statement"
  2. 2
    Select Bank Account
  3. 3
    Enter Statement Date and Closing Balancefrom your bank statement.
  4. 4
    Add Statement LinesEnter each transaction from your bank statement (description, date, debit or credit amount).
  5. 5

Matching Transactions

For each bank statement line, the system suggests matching ERP payments. Click "Match" next to a suggestion to confirm the match. Manually matched lines turn green. When all lines are matched and the balance agrees, click "Reconcile" to close the reconciliation.

Tip: If a bank line has no match, it may be an unrecorded payment. Record the payment in the Payments module first, then return to reconciliation.

8 Approvals & Workflow

The Approvals module manages multi-step approval workflows. Leave requests, expense claims, purchase orders, invoices, and other documents can be routed through configurable approval chains.

Viewing Approvals

The Approvals page has two tabs:

  • My Pending: Approvals where you are the next approver. The count badge shows how many are waiting for your action.
  • All Approvals: A full list of all approval requests in the system with status and history (visible to managers and admins).

Approving or Rejecting a Request

  1. 1
    Click a rowto open the step detail panel on the right, showing all approval steps and who has acted.
  2. 2
    Click "Approve" or "Reject"from either the row action buttons or the detail panel.
  3. 3
    Add Comments(optional but recommended, especially for rejections).
  4. 4
    Click "Confirm"Your decision is recorded with a timestamp. If there are more approvers in the chain, the next approver is notified.

Bulk Approve

On the "All Approvals" tab, check multiple rows and click "Approve Selected" to bulk-approve multiple pending requests in one action.

Approval Steps Panel

When you click an approval row, a panel slides in showing each step in the workflow. Steps show:

  • Approver name and role
  • Status: Pending Pending, Approved Approved, or Rejected Rejected
  • Timestamp and comments from when the action was taken

Filtering and Exporting

On the All Approvals tab, filter by status (Pending, Approved, Rejected, Cancelled). Select rows and click "Export Selected" to download a CSV.

Exchange Rates

Admin Settings → Exchange Rates

If your company transacts in multiple currencies, configure exchange rates here. Rates are used when converting foreign currency invoices to your base currency for the general ledger.

Adding an Exchange Rate

  1. 1
    Click "Add Rate"
  2. 2
    Select Currencye.g. EUR, GBP, AED
  3. 3
    Enter RateHow many units of your base currency equal one unit of the foreign currency. e.g. if base = USD and you enter EUR rate = 1.08, then €1 = $1.08.
  4. 4
    Set Effective Date
  5. 5
    Save

Update exchange rates regularly (daily or weekly) to keep your foreign currency balances accurate. The system uses the most recent rate that is on or before the transaction date.

Quick Reference — Status Codes

Document Statuses

Draft — Editable, not in ledger
Posted — In ledger, immutable
Approved — Reviewed & confirmed
Paid — Fully settled
Partially Paid — Part payment received
Overdue — Past due date
Cancelled — Voided with reversal
Rejected — Declined by approver

Key Permissions

View — Read-only access
Create — Make new records
Edit — Modify draft records
Delete — Remove records permanently
Post — Commit to the general ledger
Approve — Approve workflow requests

Sales cycle: Lead → Customer → Quote → Order → Invoice → Payment

Payroll cycle: Structure → Generate → Approve → Paid
